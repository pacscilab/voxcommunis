Background

Language family: Afro-Asiatic / Chadic / West Chadic / West Chadic A / West Chadic A.1

  • Hausa speakers are mostly found in southern Niger and northern Niger. The language is used as a lingua franca by non-native speakers in most of northern Nigeria, southern Niger, northern Cameroon, northern Ghana, northern Benin, northern Togo, southern Chad and parts of Sudan.
  • Writing system in CV: Boko (latin based and different by country fr/en influence) Neither length nor tone are written and thus also omitted in the G2P.

Pipeline performance:

  • Geminate /n/ and /m/. We require an executive decision about gemminate consonants and vowels. Always merge or keep intact. Depending on the G2P the decision may already have been made to concatenate geminates...
  • All in all there is a time-lag for all consonants to start slightly later than they should and end somewhat after they should have (quite consistently between 0.02s and 0.03s). All cannibalize a bit on the vowels, but especially /d/ which in turn eats up a substantial part of any subsequent V.
  • All in all the G2P looks quite believable in terms of consonants. (Does not take into account stuff like _#b | n -> m but the labels are consistent)

Pipeline performance:

  • There is generally a lot going on with the vowels which we cannot handle: Measures which depend on a correct label won't give adequate results.
  • In many analyses, Hausa has diphtongs. Our G2P does not support this.
  • Many vowel labels do mismatch with the pronunciation. Two possible explanations. (i) dialectal variation. Hausa is a lingua franca across several countries and dialects are to be expected. (ii) Deficient writing system. The Latin based system can write tones, but usually does not. What I hear as a different vowel is a high/low or long/short problem, but underlyingly the labels are correct.
